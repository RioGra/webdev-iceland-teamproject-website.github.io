Blue Lagoon

Want to visit a well-known tourist destination, a geothermal spa that is renowned for its stunning scenery milky blue waters that is full of minerals and healing properties? Check out the Blue Lagoon, located in the South-Western parts of Iceland. Established in 1981, this destination gained popularity due to its vivid blue water and incredible volcanic scenery and let us not forget its amazing skin treatment, guests are offered incredible skin treatment such as silica mud and algae masks as part of their Blue Lagoon experience. Who doesn't want great skin? It is open all year round, but the best time to go is during the summer, when the days are sunny and warm.

Jökulsárlón Glacier

Did you know that freshwater and saltwater coexist in the Jökulsárlón Glacier, which is connected to the ocean? This explains the glaciers distinct colour! This well know glacial lagoon is located in the southeast and is mostly known for its breathtaking scenery of floating icebergs and ice that are almost 1000 years old. It is a site that is truly a must see, so if you are arounds the the south of Vatnajokull check it out, and as a bonus, it’s also one of the top places to watch the northern lights. Imagine the photographs you'll take! For the best experience of these icebergs, it is best to go during the summer or early autumn.

Gull Foss Waterfall

Gull Foss, sometimes know as the “Golden Waterfall” is one of the most well-known and photographed waterfalls in Iceland. It is located near the Golden Circle tourism circuit in the southwest. The capital of Iceland is only two hours away from GullFoss Waterfall! GullFoss offers stunning views of the waterfall and canyon and for those looking for an excellent place to hike, there are a number of walking pathways and viewing platforms waiting for you. Summer months are ideal for visiting because of the longer days and temperatures however, in the winter; the waterfall can get snow-covered and is partially frozen, resulting in stunning image reminiscent of a winter wonderland.

Reynisfjara

Reynisfjara is a very famous black sand beach on Iceland’s South Coast. Many believe that Reynisfjara is the most stunning black sand beaches in Iceland because of its massive basalt stacks, crashing Atlantic waves and not to forget, it’s incredible views. Fun Fact: Reynisfjara is actually a very popular location for films or TV shows like Game of Thrones and Star Wars! It is recommended that visitors are to be careful while visiting Reynisfjara black beach, always maintain a safe distance of at least 30 meters and to never turn their backs on the waves. If you do plan on going, summer would be the best season not only for the warm temperatures but for the puffin community!! They are so cute!

Skogafoss Waterfall

Located on the Skógá River in the southern part of Iceland, Skógafoss waterfall is one of the country’s most magnificent sites for visitors. Visitors are able to approach the water’s wall directly due to how flat the land underneath the waterfall is. So if you plan to visit in the summer, it will be very tempting to get drenched with the waterfalls water. Fun Fact! Did you know that the first Viking settler of the region was Prasi Porolfsson and supposedly buried a chest filled with treasure behind the waterfall? During the summer season would be ideal to visit but for those looking for a site that appears to have been taken right out of a fantasy book, visiting in winter would is a must. Plus for those who are lucky, you can witness the Northern Lights! Just make sure to pack a really warm coat!

Westfjords

One Of the most breathtaking and incredible areas of Iceland, featuring untouched scenery, winding fjords and small, charming communities, this region is unlike any other. This area is often overlooked due to its distance from other popular destinations in Iceland bit the Westfjords is filled with natural treasures! There are many places for visitors to explore and with the minimal tourist traffic; Westfjords is a fantastic place to visit. Keep the following locations in mind if you do plan of visiting: Raudasandur Beach, Hornstrandir Nature Reserve, Latrabjarg Cliffs, and Dynjandi Waterfall. Do remember that Westfjords is only really reachable during the summer seasons.
